Surface Hydrology Viewer ALPHA

Welcome to the Surface Hydrology Viewer!

This is a web application designed to help producers, private landowners, and biologists to identify and track long-term patterns in surface water availability across Kansas. This website allows you to explore surface water data in your browser or download and work with the data directly in a GIS.

Tips on usage :
  • You can navigate the map by left-clicking and holding with your mouse and then moving the mouse. If you are using a mobile device, you can accomplish the same gesture by pressing and drag the screen. You can also use the arrow keys on your keyboard.
  • You can toggle the display layer(s) for the “most recent wet scene” and the “long-term surface hydrology” products by using the icons in the legend on the lower-left hand side of the screen.
  • You can zoom in-and-out using the wheel on your mouse, or the + and - icons located in the lower-right of the screen.
  • You can zoom to the current geographic location of your device by clicking the control button in the lower-right hand side of the screen.
  • You can zoom to an area of interest that is cached on Google Maps using the control button in the lower-right hand side of the screen.
  • If you are using a desktop computer, you can use the drawing controls located at the top of the screen to add markers or shapes around an area of interest. After you’ve delineated an area, you can export map information by right-clicking on the viewer and using the context-menu to export features. By default, this will generate a comma-separated file (CSV) with summary data that you can download and open in spreadsheet software like LibreOffice, Excel, or ‘R’.
  • You can also use the right-click context menu to remove all features on the map, as well as hide interface elements like the legend and this help window.
